# Stakeholder Register Data Rules

## What NOT to include in stakeholder descriptions

Stakeholder entries in plans (SMP, Resource Plan, Communication Plan) must be concise. The following types of internal information were explicitly rejected by the user:

| Rejected pattern | Example | Why |
|---|---|---|
| CV status | "CV submitted", "CV via DS", "CV approved by CG Jun 24" | Internal HR detail, not relevant to CG |
| PQD status | "PQD to be submitted", "PQD under submission" | Procurement tracking, not stakeholder info |
| Target dates | "Target: CP-2 Decision", "Target: CP-1 Mobilisation" | Internal programme dates |
| Reference codes | "Per KP Register Rev C02" | Document reference belongs in footnotes, not stakeholder cells |
| PO/fee details | "PO issued Jun 21 - kick-off Jun 25", "Fee 40,527 approved - Julie Riley" | Commercial terms, not stakeholder role info |
| Status updates | "Pending - waiting object research from client" | Internal progress note |
| Agreement history | "agreed 18-May-26" | Historical note, not current role info |

## What TO include

- Role title (e.g. "Project Director", "MEP Design")
- Person or firm name (e.g. "Eng. Waris Sultan", "AD Engineering")
- Approval status if relevant (e.g. "Approved", "TBC", "TBD")
- One-line scope summary without internal tracking detail

## Examples

Good: `AD Engineering - MEP Design | Approved`
Bad: `AD Engineering - MEP Design | PO issued Jun 21 - kick-off Jun 25 - agreed 18-May-26`

Good: `Eng. Waris Sultan`
Bad: `Eng. Waris Sultan - CV approved - Exhibitions PD from 13-Jun`

Good: `TBC`
Bad: `TBC - Target: CP-2 Decision - Per KP Register Rev C02`

## When to verify

Check every stakeholder entry against the specialist register
(`Technical_Office/Specialist_Management/specialist_register.md`) before writing.
The specialist register has the authoritative role-to-person mapping.

# CG Correspondence Best Practices — Aseer Museum

## Core Principles

### 1. Don't Ask Logic Questions
Never ask CG to confirm something that is obvious from the project context. Examples of what NOT to ask:
- "Should photos and frames go on walls?" — obviously yes
- "Do wall-hung objects need showcases?" — obviously no
- "Should we confirm the display method for every object?" — only if genuinely ambiguous

**Rule of thumb:** If a site engineer would know the answer without asking, do not put it in a formal letter to CG. It makes you look inexperienced and wastes everyone's time.

### 2. Flag Data Errors in CG's Own Documents
When CG sends a schedule with incorrect data (e.g., stone dimensions 150x60cm vs actual 320x150cm), flag it immediately. This is NOT a logic question — it's a data error in their deliverable. If you don't flag it before starting studies, CG will say "why didn't you tell us earlier" after you've done the work on the wrong basis.

**Valid questions to ask CG:**
- Contradictory color coding (both "selected" and "removed")
- TBC objects with no gallery assignment
- Dimensions that don't match site reality
- Reference numbers that don't correspond to any gallery (e.g., 12.65)
- Showcase types that are clearly wrong for the object size

### 3. Don't Frame Arguments That Can Be Used Against You
Avoid language that opens a debate about who is late or what stage was approved:
- ❌ "Changes after Stage 4 design approval" — CG can counter "you were already late"
- ✅ "As previously raised in our RFIs, we proceeded with drawings per your instructions. The revised schedule introduces changes that require additional studies."

Keep it factual. Don't open a debate about stages, approvals, or responsibility for delays.

### 4. Separate Threads for Different Issues
| Issue type | Send to | When |
|------------|---------|------|
| Blocking data issues (color coding, TBC items) | CG via PM | First — these are gates |
| Technical clarifications (OB218 orientation, 12.65 location) | CG separately | After blocking issues resolved |
| NRS coordination questions | NRS directly | As they arise |

**Never merge blocking issues with technical clarifications in one email.** CG will use the volume of questions to delay responding to the blocking ones.

### 5. Align with NRS Before Sending to CG
Always send draft correspondence to:
1. **Project Manager (Adel/Waris)** — for approval
2. **NRS (Jim)** — for alignment

Benefits:
- NRS backs your position when CG responds
- PM sees a united front
- CG cannot divide and conquer
- Draft can be adjusted before going official

**Confirmed workflow (from user):** Draft → PM for review → NRS for alignment → CG. Do not skip steps.

### 6. Acknowledge Receipt Without Commitment
When receiving a revised document from CG:
- Use "we confirm receipt" — not "we accept"
- Add "without prejudice to our rights to claim additional time and cost" if changes are significant
- Do NOT start work until the design basis is complete and commercial impact is agreed
- A simple "we acknowledge receipt" can be interpreted as acceptance — be explicit about preserving your rights

### 7. State Commercial Impact Upfront
Before starting any new study or redesign requested by CG:
- Flag that this is a change to employer requirements
- State that you will assess impact on cost and programme
- Do not proceed until the basis is complete and commercial terms are agreed
- Your leverage is highest BEFORE you start work — once you instruct NRS to redesign, the VO becomes a negotiation instead of a pre-condition

### 8. Keep a Paper Trail
- Every verbal instruction from CG should be confirmed in writing
- Every RFI should have a deadline for response
- If CG doesn't respond by the deadline, the delay is theirs — document it

## Common Traps

| Trap | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Asking CG to confirm obvious things | Makes you look inexperienced | Only ask about genuine ambiguities |
| Merging blocking issues with technical questions | CG delays responding to blocking ones | Keep separate threads |
| Sending to CG before aligning with NRS | CG can divide and conquer | Send draft to PM + NRS first |
| Acknowledging receipt = accepting scope | Loses your right to claim | Use "without prejudice" language |
| Starting work before commercial terms agreed | Lose leverage for VO | Don't proceed until basis is complete |
| Framing arguments about stages/approvals | CG can counter "you were late" | Keep it factual — "we proceeded per your instructions" |
| Not flagging data errors in CG's documents | CG says "why didn't you tell us" after you study wrong basis | Flag immediately — it's their error, not your question |

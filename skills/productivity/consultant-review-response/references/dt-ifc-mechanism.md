# DtIFC Mechanism — ER §2.4 Verbatim Text + Register Template

## 1. Verbatim ER §2.4 (Aseer Museum, Contractors Employers Requirements)

### §2.4 Approval Framework (Full Text, Pages 13-15)

> This section covers approval procedures for the Design, Construction, and the Testing & Commissioning phases of the Contract. Reviews by the PMC are only for contractual conformance with the ERs. These reviews do not constitute any form of technical review, which shall be undertaken by the Contractor's nominated designer under this D&B contract.
> 
> For expediency, the design phase for this D&B contract **shall** be combined into a single Design Development & Construction Documentation phase. The content of these submissions is described in this section.
> 
> The Contractor **may** consider some elements of the design to be able to proceed straight to IFC stage. **Any elements considered as such are required to be listed for the PMC and MoC consideration and approval prior to proceeding any further. No elements of the design may proceed straight to IFC stage without this review.**

### §2.4.A — Review Period

> The review period, where applicable, shall be **14 calendar days**, unless otherwise specified.
> 
> Approval means a "no objection" has been provided for aesthetics and conformance with the ERs. Even though an item may have been "approved," all design liability shall be retained by the Contractor.

### §2.4.B — Design Certification Requirement

> Prior to any submittal being made to the PMC for conformance review, all design and documentation must have been:
> a. Checked by a suitably qualified and experienced design professional
> b. Reviewed for quality assurance purposes
> c. Approved for issued-for-construction (IFC) by the Contractor
>
> With the following certification wording:
> 
> *"We hereby certify that: The submitted documents, and design contained therein, have been reviewed and approved by a suitably qualified, experienced and registered engineer, architect and/or design professional; comply with all obligations of the Contract, including but not limited to the Employer's Requirements; and The works as designed, and when duly constructed in accordance with the design, will be fit for the purpose intended, as specified in the Employer's Requirements."*

### §2.4.D — Design Phase Submittals (Material Sequencing)

> Each submission will follow a consistent naming and numbering convention to enable a clear connection between the contents of each submittal.
> 
> In order to undertake an orderly and efficient design review, the Contractor shall submit a **complete IFC document list at the commencement of the design development phase**.
> 
> **Product / material submittal shall be included in the overall Works package IFC documentation submission, and shall not be submitted independently to the IFC documentation package.** All products and materials proposed by the Contractor shall be reviewed and approved by the Contractor's Designer.

## 2. Verbatim SoW §6.1 (Sub-Stage Requirement)

> **6.1 Process**
>
> This stage will be divided into **50% and 90% sub-stages** with the Contractor responsible for submitting and obtaining approval on 50% (interim) and (draft final) 90% deliverables from the MoC before proceeding into the next substage. Each deliverable is required to be presented in a meeting to the MoC.
>
> The deliverables will only be considered 100% complete when they have been formally accepted by the PMC and/or MoC.

## 3. Verbatim SoW §6.5 (Review SLA)

> The Contractor is to allow for a **minimum of 14 working days for stage-end reviews** by the MoC and/or PMC. It is at MoC's discretion as to whether this time is required at all or at each and every part of the works.

## 4. Verbatim SoW §6.6 — §6.7 (Conformance-Only Reviews)

> **6.6 Review and Approvals** — Reviews at all stages by the PMC are only for contractual conformance with the Employer's Requirements (ERs). These reviews do not constitute any form of technical review, which shall be undertaken by the Contractor's nominated designer under their Design and Build contract.
>
> **6.7 Review and Approval Procedures** — The review period, where applicable, shall be 14 calendar days, unless otherwise specified.

## 5. Verbatim SoW §6.9 (IFC Document List)

> In order to undertake an orderly and efficient design review, the Contractor shall submit a complete IFC document list at the commencement of the design development phase and update it during the construction document phases of the project.

## 6. Verbatim SoW §6.11 (Product Data / Material Submittal)

> The Contractor shall compile product data into a single submittal for each package of the Works. Product / material submittal elements shall be based on the requirements stipulated in MasterSpec for the relevant specification sections.
>
> Prior to making the submittal, the Contractor shall prepare a Product / Material Submittal Schedule listing: material/product name, **relevant drawing reference numbers of the Contractor's IFC Documentation package**, relevant specification section numbers cross-referenced to the Contractor's IFC Documentation package, manufacturer's name, etc.

## 7. Conflict Resolution Table: ER §2.4 vs SoW §6.1

| Issue | ER §2.4 | SoW §6.1 | Resolution |
|-------|---------|-----------|------------|
| Design phase structure | Combined single DD+CD phase | 50%/90%/100% sub-stages | ER §2.4 combined-phase mandate + SoW §6.2 (ERs are detailed reference) → Combined phase controls |
| DtIFC permission | Element-level, subject to register approval | Silent | ER §2.4 provides the mechanism; SoW §6.1 sub-stages default applies if DtIFC register rejected |
| Review period (stage-end) | 14 calendar days (§2.4.A) | 14 working days (§6.5) | Use **14 working days** for 50%/90% gate reviews — SoW §6.5 is specific to stage-end reviews |
| Review period (CG conformance) | 14 calendar days | 14 calendar days (SoW §6.7 mirrors) | Both agree: 14 calendar days for conformance reviews |
| Material submittal timing | Included in IFC documentation package (§2.4.D) | References IFC Drawing numbers (§6.11) | Both agree: materials reference IFC documentation — submittal after design drawings exist |
| IFC Document List | — | Required at design commencement (§6.9) | SoW §6.9 controls — must be submitted |

## 9. Classification Test: Is a Discipline DtIFC-Viable?

Ask these 5 questions for each discipline. If ALL answers are "no" or "not applicable", the discipline is DtIFC-viable:

| # | Question | If YES → not DtIFC-viable |
|---|---------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Does the design require client-supplied content (text, copyright, media) to reach IFC level? | Not viable — content dependency |
| 2 | Does the design require employer-approved exhibit/curatorial direction before dimensions are fixed? | Not viable — curatorial dependency |
| 3 | Does the design involve MoC-specific branding, messaging, or narrative that cannot use placeholders? | Not viable — narrative dependency |
| 4 | Is the discipline structural or MEP-related (non-content)? | Viable — no content dependency |
| 5 | Is the firm already appointed and mobilized? | If not appointed yet → risk but still viable for DtIFC register, with procurement running in parallel |

### Expected Classification Results (Museum Fit-Out)

| Discipline | Viable for DtIFC? | Reason |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| Architectural & Interior Design | **YES** | No content dependency; plans/elevations/sections are spatial, not narrative |
| Structural Engineering | **YES** | Load calculations, reinforcement design — independent of exhibition content |
| MEP Design (HVAC/Plumb/Fire) | **YES** | System sizing, duct routing, pipe layouts — independent of exhibition content |
| Electrical & ELV Design | **YES** | Panel schedules, cable routing, distribution — independent of exhibition content |
| Exhibition Lighting | **YES** | Fixture selection and placement based on spatial plan and conservation requirements — doesn't need exhibition text |
| BIM Federation & Coordination | **YES** | Process-driven, not content-driven |
| Scenography | **NO** | Requires exhibition text, object selection, narrative direction |
| Graphics & Wayfinding | **NO** | Requires exhibition text, copyright clearance, wayfinding strategy from MoC |
| AV Systems | **NO** | Platform spec at 50% is possible, but content (media software) is MoC-supplied — 90% and IFC depend on it |
| Showcase Design | **NO** | Depends on object dimensions, conservation requirements, mounts — MoC-provided |

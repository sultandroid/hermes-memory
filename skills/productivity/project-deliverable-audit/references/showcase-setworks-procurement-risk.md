## 2. Procurement Risks

### PR-01: No appointed showcase manufacturer
All DT drawings are drawn by "AV" (architect/designer CMS). The DT_4000 family sheet states: "specialist manufacturer must advise on glass, mounts, hinges, locks, climate control, lighting." No manufacturer appointment evidenced. Bespoke museum-grade showcase lead times are typically 20-30 weeks.

### PR-02: Glasbau Hahn scope unconfirmed
The only GH reference is in the Exhibit Schedule (01-AS/11-Exhibits, line 69): "Showcase IDs cluster (e.g. 01.02_SW_01-03) directly inform showcase fab schedule + Glasbau Hahn quotation interface." No GH scope document, specification, or standards input exists in the drawing set. A quotation interface is NOT an appointment.

### PR-03: All 8 showcase type drawings are Rev "/" (information only)
Drawing register DT sheet shows all DT_400x at Rev "/". Cannot be used for procurement. Full re-issue to Rev C required.

### PR-04: G4 (Saudi Art) Showcase Locations GA missing
Drawing register lists G4_0005, but the PDF does not exist in the filesystem. The folder jumps from G4_0004 (Setwork) to G4_0006 (Graphics). G4 holds object IDs OB007-OB092 with extensive artefacts needing showcases.

## 3. Dimensional/Access Risks

### LG-01: T1 (8300mm) too large for any standard opening
Standard door openings ~900-1800mm wide. T1 must be assembled in-situ within the gallery recess. This requires coordinated wall construction sequencing. Risk: if the wall is built before the case design is finalised, fit tolerances may fail.

### LG-02: T2 (2950mm tall) exceeds standard door height
Standard doors ~2100mm. Even service doors rarely exceed 2700mm. T2 cannot be delivered assembled through any standard doorway. Options:
- In-situ assembly (specialist joiners on site)
- Goods lift with >=3000mm clear height (verify existence)
- Crane through facade opening before envelope close

### LG-03: No delivery route study
No door schedule, lift capacity/size study, or corridor width analysis exists. T1 (8.3m), T2 (2.95m), T3 (5.85m), T6A/B (2.45m) all need route verification. Basement (BF) galleries accessed via a single replacement stair (escalator pit infill) — no goods lift identified.

## 4. Interface/Coordination Risks

### IN-01: Showcase microclimate vs HVAC — no coordination
Only T1 notes "climate: passive (specialist to confirm)." The other 7 types have no environmental specification. Interface statement says "MEP contractor designs in next stage (D&B)" with only general comfort criteria. Sensitive artefacts (textiles in T3, archaeology in G12, calligraphy in G11) need controlled microclimates.

### IN-02: Showcase lighting integration gap
Lighting specification confirms "showcase lighting via rotary dimmers inside showcases" (DALI control). Lighting drawings exist (ZNA SH drawings) but without manufacturer input. Risks: incompatible integration with glass/framing, heat buildup in sealed cases, electrical certification split.

### IN-03: No power/data requirements per showcase type
DT_400x drawings do not specify power, data, or lighting per type. General LL/HL Power GAs exist per gallery but don't show showcase-specific feeds. LV contractor cannot price containment; manufacturer cannot integrate lighting.

### IN-04: Showcase weights unknown — no structural check
Structural report and interface statement address art commissions (2400kg stained glass, 400kg marble) but not showcase weights. Floor loading = 4 kN/m2 (~400 kg/m2). T1 at 8.3m2 footprint holding heavy artefacts could easily exceed this. No structural verification.

### IN-05: EX2 setworks based on invalidated design
EX2 Stair-Ramp setworks (EX_2000-2001) are flagged "In Abeyance" in the structural report — existing RC ramp at/near capacity, requiring full demolition and new steel/composite deck. The EX2 folder under GF galleries is entirely empty. Setworks drawings are based on a now-invalid design.

## 6. Findings from EX2 Stair-Ramp Setworks

The EX_2000 (EX2.01_SW_01-2) and EX_2001 (EX2.03_SW_01-4) are setworks drawings for:
- EX2.01 — External Seating zone (stepped seating, planters)
- EX2.03 — Museum Entrance zone (bilingual signage band, threshold)

These are architectural exhibition structures (setworks), NOT showcases. The setworks detail:
- Stepped seating integrated with retaining walls (950mm seat heights)
- Ramp runs (4181mm wide, 25805mm long, 3100mm rise)
- Entrance signage band (~8000 x 2000mm)
- Bilingual Arabic + English signage

The structural report flags:
- EX2 ramp beams at/near capacity — load-credit check fails
- Existing RC ramp must be demolished; new steel + composite-deck ramp with lightweight concrete infill
- External portion on pad foundations
- "In Abeyance" designation means the design direction is unconfirmed

The EX2 folder under GF galleries (6_Galleries/GF/EX2) is entirely empty — no GA plans, no setwork drawings, no power/data drawings.

## 8. Key Recommendations

1. **Immediately confirm Glasbau Hahn (or alternative) appointment** and clarify whether their standard range covers the 8 types or bespoke fabrication is needed.
2. **Commission a Showcase Procurement Package** including per-type weight/structural calculations, power/data/lighting/HVAC interface schedules, delivery route study, and on-site assembly vs factory-assembled decision per type.
3. **Close the G4 documentation gap** — issue G4_0005 Showcase Locations GA.
4. **Baseline the EX2 ramp design** before proceeding with setworks detailing.
5. **Define showcase environmental requirements** (temp, RH, pollutant filtration) per gallery/object type and submit to MEP D&B contractor as an interface brief.
6. **Verify floor loading capacity** for heaviest-loaded showcases, especially T1 and T2.

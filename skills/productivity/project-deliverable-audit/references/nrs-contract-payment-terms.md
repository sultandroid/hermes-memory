### What This Means

- The contract says **"Monthly Instalments"** for ALL stages — not milestone-gated or EV-linked
- Art. 11.2: *"The Designer shall submit invoices for fees in accordance with an invoicing schedule that the parties shall agree in advance of commencing the work."*
- Art. 11.3: Payment due 14 days after invoice receipt

### Key Contract Articles for Negotiation Leverage

| Article | Text | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **11.2** | Invoicing schedule "agreed in advance" | Stage 5 schedule was never formally agreed — you can argue it's still open |
| **5.1** | Stage 5 = "off-site fabrication review" of submittals against approved IFC | No IFC = no Stage 5 work to review = paying for work that can't happen |
| **4.7** | IFC delivery within 8 weeks (by ~April 2026) | NRS has not delivered IFC — blocked by SI007 |
| **18** | Amendments must be in writing | Any restructured payment plan needs a formal written amendment |
| **20.1** | 1%/week delay penalty, max 10% | Exists but rarely enforceable due to Art. 20.2 |
| **20.2** | No penalty for Customer-caused delays | NRS will argue SI007 blocks them → Samaya-caused |
| **14.2** | If Samaya terminates → NRS gets "full fees for actual stage" | Expensive to terminate |
| **14.3** | If NRS terminates → paid only for services actually rendered | NRS would lose unpaid Stage 5-6 money |

### Recommended Approach

1. Acknowledge the contract's monthly language (Art. 11)
2. Counter with Art. 11.2 (never-agreed Stage 5 schedule) and Art. 5.1 (IFC dependency)
3. Propose a **written amendment** (Art. 18) that replaces "monthly instalments" with a hybrid:
   - Small monthly retainer (SAR 20-30K) to keep NRS staff available
   - Balance released against IFC + specialist package milestones

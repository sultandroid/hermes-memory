# CG Material Submittal Pushback — Response Pattern

When CG (e.g., Mansour Alrezeni) pushes back on a material submittal, claiming insufficient or incorrect samples were provided, use this triage and response pattern.

## Trigger signals
- CG says "only X sample(s) matching the specified finish received"
- CG says "remaining materials are alternatives, not the specified finish"
- CG requests additional manufacturers for a single-source material
- CG disputes the count or type of submitted samples

## Workflow

### 1. Audit what was actually submitted
Before responding, verify the actual submission record:
- Check the submittal folder (e.g., `09_Submittals/MA-0007_Rev00/`) for submitted samples
- Check the CR sheet for the itemized list
- Check the subcontractor's letter (e.g., GBH Letter 002) for their position

### 2. Structure the response

**Clarify the submission count:**
- State exactly how many samples matching the specified finish were submitted (e.g., "Three (3) different patinated brass effect samples were submitted under MA-0007 Rev.00")
- State these are currently with CG for reference

**Frame alternatives as supplementary, not replacements:**
- Each alternative gets its own bullet with context (who recommended it, why it was included)
- Explicitly state: "offered as supplementary options to expand CG's choices, not to replace the specified finish"
- Never let CG frame alternatives as the primary submission

### 3. Propose split-track approval

When the material testing timeline (Oddy, certifications) threatens to delay other deliverables:

| Track | Content | Status |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Track A** | Shop drawings & other materials (MA-0006) | Proceed independently — finish is surface-only, doesn't affect fabrication |
| **Track B** | Specified finish (MA-0007) | Testing in progress, alternative development continues |

**Key arguments for Track A:**
- Subcontractor confirms the finish is a surface treatment only — does not affect fabrication or production
- Separating prevents the testing timeline from delaying the critical path
- CR sheet reflects this approach and is the main deliverable

### 4. Reference the CR sheet as the main deliverable

The email is a **support document** — the CR sheet is the formal response. Structure:
- Short cover email (3-4 paragraphs max)
- CR sheet attached with full itemized responses
- Email references the CR sheet by date and revision

### 5. Reference the risk register

If the material issue is already tracked in the risk register (e.g., PRR-PRC-05 for patinated brass), mention the risk ID in the email so CG sees it's being managed.

## Email template structure

```
Subject: RE: [Submittal Ref] / [Material Name]

Dear Eng. [Name],

Good day.

Regarding your email, please find below the clarification on the submitted samples under [Submittal Ref]:

**[Material] samples submitted:**
- [Count] different [finish] samples were submitted under [Submittal Ref Rev.XX]
- Currently with CG for reference

**Alternatives submitted (supplementary, not replacements):**
- [Alternative 1] — [context: who recommended, why included]
- [Alternative 2] — [context]

Both alternatives were offered as supplementary options to expand CG's choices, not to replace the specified [material].

**Current status:**
As detailed in the CR Sheet ([ref], dated [date]):
- [Testing status — what's in progress, expected results date]
- [Supplier position — e.g., only one global supplier exists]
- [Proposed split-track approach]

**Request for CG acceptance of the split approach:**

We formally request CG to accept separating the approval process into two independent tracks:

Track A — Shop Drawings & [Other Materials]:
- [Material] is a surface finish only and does not affect fabrication
- [Subcontractor] has confirmed this position
- Proceed with shop drawing approval and [other submittal] independently

Track B — [Specified Finish]:
- [Testing] in progress — results expected [date]
- [Alternative] in development — sample within [timeline]
- No impact on shop drawing or other material approval timeline

This split prevents the [material] testing timeline from delaying the critical path for [fabrication/production].

The CR Sheet ([ref]) reflecting this approach has been submitted. We await CG's acceptance.

Best regards,
Eng. Sultan Issa
Technical Office Manager
Samaya Investment Company
```

## Pitfalls
- **Don't let CG frame alternatives as the primary submission** — always clarify they are supplementary options
- **Don't argue about the count** — state the facts clearly, don't get defensive
- **Don't make the email the main deliverable** — the CR sheet is the formal response, the email is context
- **Don't forget the risk register** — if the issue is tracked, reference the risk ID
- **Don't propose split-track without subcontractor confirmation** — get written confirmation that the finish doesn't affect fabrication first (e.g., GBH Letter 002)

## Interpreting "outside our scope" statements from NRS

**NRS (Nissen Richards Studio)** is the exhibition design consultant on contract A2742. Their scope covers architectural/interior design, setworks, and joinery (T2-08, T2-14). **They do NOT** cover:

- AV / IT systems (T2-10 → AVD / T2-09 → Rawasin)
- Interactives (T2-09 → Rawasin)
- Lighting design (T2-06 → ZNA)
- Showcases (T2-07 → Glasbau Hahn)
- Conservation (T2-20 → CON)
- CMS software (T2-11 → TBC)

When NRS says "X is outside our scope," the correct response is:

1. Check the T2 table — is another sub already assigned? ✅ → Route to them
2. If no T2 assignee exists → this IS a procurement gap, flag to PM
3. **Do not** conclude it's a Samaya direct responsibility without checking T2 first

## Key pitfall

**Do not label "outside X's scope" as a procurement gap without checking the T2 table first.** What looks like an uncovered scope item is often already assigned to another subcontractor. The user will correct you — and they know their structure better than you do.

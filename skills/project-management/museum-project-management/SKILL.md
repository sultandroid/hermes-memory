# Museum Project Management — Contractor's Knowledge Base

Load this skill when the task involves: contract review, claim preparation, variation assessment, programme/EOT analysis, museum fitout planning, procurement strategy, or any project management task on a museum/contractor project.

## 1. Contract Administration — Notice Deadlines (CRITICAL)

| Contract | Claim Notice | Variation | Concealed Conditions | Dispute |
|----------|-------------|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| **FIDIC Red Book 2005** | 28 days (Clause 20.1) — absolute bar | Engineer instructs (Clause 13) | "As soon as practicable" (Clause 4.12) | Engineer decision → 56d amicable → ICC arbitration |
| **AIA A201-2017** | 21 days certified mail | Proceed = waiver trap (§7.4) | 14 days (§3.7.4) | Mandatory mediation first |

**FIDIC 28-day trap**: Missing the 28-day notice under Clause 20.1 forfeits ALL entitlement to EOT and additional payment. This is the single most critical provision. Always issue a preliminary notice within 28 days of becoming aware of the event, even if full details aren't ready.

**AIA proceed = waiver trap** (§7.4): If the contractor proceeds with a change without a signed Change Order or CCD, they waive the right to additional time or cost. Never start work without signed documentation.

## 2. Museum Environmental Specs (from Thomson + BS EN 16893)

| Parameter | Target | Contractor Action |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Light (sensitive objects)** | 50 lux max | UV filters on ALL sources, dichroic reflectors, separate lamps from cases |
| **Light (moderate objects)** | 200 lux max | Same as above |
| **Relative Humidity** | 50% ±5% (verify per zone) | Vapour barriers, HVAC with BOTH humidification AND dehumidification, airtight case construction |
| **Temperature** | 19-24°C | No thermal bridging, high-performance insulation, vibration isolation |
| **Air quality** | Multi-stage + activated carbon | Low-VOC materials, positive pressure, intakes away from pollution |
| **Airtightness** | <0.5 m³/h·m² at 50 Pa | Continuous vapour barrier, sealed penetrations |
| **Fire protection** | ASD + gas/water-mist preferred | Pre-action sprinklers if wet, storage = separate fire compartment |

**Key principle**: BS EN 16893 does NOT prescribe fixed numbers — the client's conservator defines permissible limits per collection zone. Always verify per zone, don't assume.

**Bake-out period**: After construction, run HVAC at elevated temperature to off-gas VOCs before collections move in. Factor this into programme.

## 3. FIDIC Key Clauses for Museum Projects

| Clause | Topic | What to Know |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 4.24 | Fossils/Antiquities | Directly relevant — archaeological finds on museum sites trigger EOT + Cost automatically |
| 4.12 | Unforeseeable Physical Conditions | Notice "as soon as practicable" (no 28-day trap). Covers unexpected ground, existing services |
| 4.18 | Environment | Strict controls — relevant near sensitive exhibits or during phased handover |
| 8.4 | Extension of Time | Concurrent delay rules apply. Must show delay is on critical path |
| 13 | Variations | Engineer can instruct; contractor can object if affects programme or outside capability |
| 14 | Payment | Monthly applications. 14.8: financing charges at 3% above central bank rate, no formal notice needed |
| 15-16 | Termination | Contractor termination (16) for non-payment after 56 days |

## 4. AIA A201-2017 Key Provisions

- **Retainage**: 5% until 50% completion, then full release
- **Consequential damages**: Mutual waiver — can't claim lost profits, but direct costs + extended overhead are claimable
- **Subcontractor flow-down**: 7-day payment obligation to subs after receiving payment
- **Stop-work for non-payment**: Permitted after proper notice
- **Phased completion**: If project has phased handover (common in museums), define substantial completion per phase

## 5. PMBOK 7th Ed — Principles & Domains

**12 Principles**: Stewardship, Team, Stakeholders, Value, Systems Thinking, Leadership, Tailoring, Quality, Complexity, Risk, Adaptability, Resilience, Change.

**8 Performance Domains** (not process groups):
1. **Stakeholder** — engage early and continuously. Museum has many: CG, NRS, GBH, end-client, conservators
2. **Team** — build shared understanding of museum-specific requirements
3. **Development Approach & Life Cycle** — predictive (design-bid-build) vs adaptive. Museum fitout often needs hybrid
4. **Planning** — WBS, schedule, cost baseline. EVM for progress measurement
5. **Project Work** — ITPs, inspections, commissioning
6. **Delivery** — quality meets acceptance criteria. Phased handover for museum zones
7. **Measurement** — EVM (SPI, CPI), KPIs, dashboards
8. **Uncertainty** — risk register, contingency, complexity management

**Tailoring**: PMBOK 7th explicitly says to tailor. For museum fitout: heavy stakeholder domain, heavy uncertainty domain, hybrid life cycle (predictive for shell, adaptive for fitout).

## 6. RIBA Plan of Work 2013

| Stage | Name | Contractor Relevance |
|-------|------|---------------------|
| 0 | Strategic Definition | Understand client's museum brief |
| 1 | Preparation & Brief | Review concept design, environmental strategy |
| 2 | Concept Design | Input on buildability, cost |
| 3 | Spatial Coordination | Detailed design coordination |
| 4 | Technical Design | Specialist subcontractor design (cases, AV, lighting) |
| 5 | Manufacturing & Construction | Site works, MEP, fitout |
| 6 | Handover | Commissioning, training, as-builts |
| 7 | In Use | POE, aftercare, defects |

**Procurement recommendation for museums**: Two-Stage Design & Build — lets specialist subcontractors (showcases, AV, lighting) design in Stage 4 while main works proceed.

**Stages 4 and 5 overlap** for specialist subcontractor design — critical for museum fitout where casework, MEP, and AV are interdependent.

## 7. Museum Fitout-Specific Considerations

- **Phased handover**: Museum zones often hand over sequentially (shell → MEP → fitout → exhibits). Define substantial completion per phase in contract
- **Specialist subcontractors**: Showcase manufacturers (GBH), AV integrators, lighting designers, conservation consultants — all need early involvement
- **MEP complexity**: Precision HVAC (RH control), redundant systems, dedicated electrical for showcases, fire suppression coordination
- **Fine arts insurance**: Contractor may need additional coverage for client's collections during fitout
- **Security**: Access control per zone, separate fire compartments, ASD detection
- **Materials**: Low-VOC, non-off-gassing, museum-grade finishes. Verify all materials against conservation requirements

## 9. Showcase Environmental Control Systems

When the task involves specifying environmental control for museum display showcases (active control, monitoring, or passive buffering), use this section.

### 9.1 Solution Tiers

| Tier | Solution | Cost Range (per showcase) | Control | Monitoring | Loan Compliance |
|------|----------|--------------------------|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Passive | Silica gel (color-indicating, rechargeable) | ~$10-15 | None — buffers RH only | None | No |
| Monitoring only | Data logger (HOBO MX1101, Testo 174H) | ~$120-150 | None | Yes — logged T/RH | Partial — data exists but no control |
| Active control | Microclimate unit (CCI RH-33 or equivalent) | ~$200-800 (Alibaba) to $1,800+ (branded) | Active T/RH via Peltier + humidifier/dehumidifier | Built-in | Yes — full compliance |

### 9.2 Quantity Calculation — CRITICAL RULE

**1 unit per showcase, regardless of volume.** Each sealed showcase is an independent microclimate. Air does not circulate between cases. A 3m-long case needs the same 1 unit as a 1m case (within typical museum showcase volumes of 0.5-3 m³).

Do NOT calculate by volume, floor area, or gallery zone for in-showcase control. Gallery-level sensors are separate.

### 9.3 Cost Estimation — Mandatory Rules

- **Do NOT estimate prices from memory.** Always mark costs as TBC unless verified from:
  - Alibaba listings (search: "museum showcase humidity controller" or "showcase microclimate controller")
  - Direct supplier quotation (CCI, RH, local KSA distributor)
  - At least 3 competitive quotes
- **Alibaba range for Chinese-manufactured units:** ~$200-800 per unit (varies by features, capacity, MOQ)
- **Branded European units (CCI, RH):** ~$1,500-2,500 per unit
- **Always include:** unit price × quantity + installation (~$100-200/unit) + commissioning + shipping + customs
- **Pitfall:** If you present unverified prices, the user will ask "are you sure?" — mark all costs TBC and state the source gap explicitly.

### 9.4 Monitoring vs Control — Distinction to Always Make

| Aspect | Monitoring (data loggers) | Active Control (microclimate units) |
|--------|--------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Does it measure T/RH? | Yes | Yes (built-in) |
| Does it adjust T/RH? | No | Yes — heating, cooling, humidification, dehumidification |
| Does it alert on breach? | Yes (with gateway) | Yes (built-in) |
| Can it replace silica gel? | No | Yes |
| Loan agreement acceptance | Partial — proves conditions but can't fix them | Full — maintains conditions within spec |

**Default recommendation for loaned artifacts:** Active control units inside showcases. Monitoring-only is insufficient because it can only report a problem, not prevent damage.

### 9.5 Response Protocol Template

When defining alert thresholds and response actions for a showcase monitoring/control system:

| Parameter | Recommended Range | Warning (Amber) | Critical (Red) |
|-----------|------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Temperature | 18-22°C | >24°C or <16°C | >26°C or <14°C |
| Relative Humidity | 45-55% (organic materials) | >60% or <40% | >65% or <35% |
| T change per hour | ±2°C | ±3°C | ±5°C |
| RH change per hour | ±5% | ±8% | ±10% |

**Response escalation:**
- **Amber:** Email + dashboard → check HVAC zone → verify showcase seal → recalibrate within 24h
- **Red:** Immediate SMS + alarm → evacuate artifacts to buffer room → shut down HVAC in zone → log incident → conservator inspects artifacts
- **Power outage:** UPS powers loggers for 72h+ continuous recording

### 9.6 Reading Frequency

| Location | Interval | Rationale |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Inside showcases | Every 5-10 min | Artifact sensitivity, rapid detection of change |
| Gallery halls | Every 15-30 min | Relatively stable, general monitoring |
| Control/storage rooms | Every 30-60 min | Stable environment |

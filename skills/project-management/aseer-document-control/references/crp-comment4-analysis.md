# CRP Comment 4 — SAMAYA Contractual Responsibility Analysis

## Session Context
Date: 2026-06-08
Document: CRP Rev C02 (MOC-ASEER-SIC-1K0-PL-0027)
CG Reviewer: Mohamed Elbaz, Acting PM (25-May-2026)

## CG Comment 4 (Verbatum)
> "For the avoidance of doubt, SAMAYA remains fully and ultimately contractually responsible for all deliverables under its designated scope of work. While SAMAYA shall be removed from this specific workload distribution table for administrative alignment, this adjustment does not waive or reduce their contractual obligations. SAMAYA must exercise full due diligence across all project domains to ensure all deliverables meet the required standards and timelines."

## Three Prongs
1. SAMAYA remains fully contractually responsible ✓ (statement of fact)
2. Remove SAMAYA from workload distribution table ⚠️ (administrative action)
3. Removal doesn't waive obligations ✓ (legal clarification)

## Problem with Prong 2

The workload distribution table in §8.1 counts R+A assignments from the RACI matrix — it's a **management visibility tool**, not a contractual document. Removing SAMAYA from it:

- Hides useful coordination data from the project team
- Makes the table incomplete (shows only CG/PMC/MoC loads without the executing party)
- Doesn't change SAMAYA's contractual responsibility one way or the other
- Addresses a misinterpretation risk, not a real compliance issue

## Recommended Approach (Not Blind Deletion)

Instead of removing SAMAYA rows, reframe the table to prevent misinterpretation:

| Original Label | Reframed Label |
|---|---|
| "Responsibility-Load Distribution" | "Communication Interaction Distribution" |
| "Active Load Bar (R + A)" | "Communication Touch-Points" |

Add explicit disclaimer:
> "This table counts communication coordination touch-points only. SAMAYA retains 100% contractual responsibility for all deliverables per §1.2."

## Resolution Status in CRP Rev C02

The C02 version kept the same §8.1 table as C01 (identical R/A counts, identical roles). The only addition was:
- A note at the bottom: "RACI load distribution does not reduce contractual liability"
- The Governing Framework strip in §1.2

**This was insufficient** — CG asked for structural change (removal), not just a disclaimer. The comment was labeled "addressed" but the actual content didn't change between revisions.

## Lesson
When a CG comment asks for a structural change (remove X), adding a Y disclaimer next to X does not address the comment. Either make the structural change or explicitly reframe why the structure is preserved and how the underlying concern is addressed.

# Contractor Correspondence Strategy — Design Change Notification

## When to Use

CG/consultant sends a revised design basis (object schedule, drawing, specification) that changes the scope after a stage gate. You need to respond without accepting liability, without starting work prematurely, and without damaging the relationship.

## Core Principles

| Principle | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| **Acknowledge receipt, not acceptance** | "We confirm receipt" ≠ "we accept this as the basis" |
| **Don't open stage/approval debates** | Never argue about whether Stage 3 was approved or who is late — it's a distraction |
| **Reference prior RFIs** | Shows you flagged the issue earlier — not a surprise |
| **State what you need before proceeding** | "We need X clarified before we can start" — puts the ball in their court |
| **Flag cost/programme impact without demanding a VO upfront** | "We will assess the impact" is softer than "we demand a VO" but preserves your position |
| **Draft to PM + aligned party first** | Get internal alignment before sending to CG |

## The "Draft to PM + Ally First" Workflow

Before sending any formal response to CG/consultant:

1. **Draft the email** with your proposed position
2. **Send to your Project Manager + an aligned party** (e.g., NRS if they share your concerns)
3. **Get their buy-in** — adjust wording if needed
4. **Only then send to CG** — now you have a united front

This prevents CG from dividing and conquering (calling NRS separately and getting a different story).

## Email Structure Template

```
Subject: [PROJECT-REF] — [Topic]: Clarifications Required Before Proceeding

Dear [Name],

We confirm receipt of [document reference/date].

As previously raised in our RFIs, [issue] required clarification. In the meantime,
we proceeded with [work] per your instructions.

The revised [document] introduces changes that require additional studies and
design revisions. Before we proceed, we need the following items clarified to
establish a complete design basis and avoid rework:

1. [Issue 1 — specific, factual]
2. [Issue 2 — specific, factual]
3. [Issue 3 — specific, factual]

Proposed Next Steps
Once the above is resolved, we will assess the full impact on design, cost, and
programme, and proceed accordingly.

We are ready to move forward once the design basis is complete.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## Key Phrases and Their Effect

| Phrase | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| "We confirm receipt" | Acknowledges delivery — no commitment |
| "Without prejudice to our rights" | Preserves your position to claim later |
| "As previously raised in our RFIs" | Shows you flagged it — not a surprise |
| "Per your instructions" | You were following orders, not late |
| "To establish a complete design basis" | Frames your request as necessary, not obstructive |
| "Avoid rework" | Shared interest — they don't want to pay twice either |
| "Assess the full impact" | Flags cost/programme without demanding a VO upfront |
| "Once the design basis is complete" | You won't start until data is ready |

## What NOT to Say

| Don't Say | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "We accept the revised schedule" | Implies scope agreed — lose leverage for VO |
| "We will proceed with studies" | Starts work without commercial agreement |
| "Stage 3 was never approved" | Opens a debate you don't need — they'll counter with "you're late" |
| "This is a variation" | Too aggressive upfront — let the facts speak |
| "You should have done X" | Blame language — damages relationship |

## When to Send the Draft to PM First

Always, when:
- The change has cost/programme impact
- You need an aligned party (NRS, subcontractor) to back your position
- The message could be seen as confrontational

Skip the draft step only when:
- The response is purely administrative (confirming a meeting date)
- The issue is trivial and you have clear authority

## Real Example (Aseer Museum — Revised Object Schedule)

**Situation:** CG sent a revised object schedule with 80 objects mapped to showcases, ~40 TBC items, and color coding errors in G4/G6. NRS had already complained about the same issues.

**Workflow:**
1. Drafted email requesting color coding fix + TBC clarification before proceeding
2. Sent draft to PM (Adel) + NRS (Jim) for review
3. Jim confirmed NRS position aligned
4. PM approved sending
5. Sent to CG — united front, no surprises

**Result:** CG could not divide and conquer. NRS backed the position. Samaya preserved its right to claim time/cost without starting work prematurely.

# Subcontractor Email Protocol

When drafting emails to or about subcontractors during appointment/contract phase.

## Core Rule

**A subcontractor's scope is defined by their own SOW — not by what other contractors don't do.**

Do NOT include explanations like "Party A does X, your scope is only Y, Party B does Z." The subcontractor only needs to know what they are responsible for delivering. Internal contracting structure between other parties (MEP contractor, BIM specialist, etc.) is not their concern.

## Do vs Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Your scope covers design services as per the approved SOW." | "You only do design because the MEP contractor handles shop drawings and installation." |
| "Please confirm acceptance of the attached SOW." | "Please confirm the scope split: you = design only, others = execution." |

## Rationale

- Including other parties' scope creates confusion and can lead to scope-creep claims
- The subcontractor may interpret "X is not your job" as "we're limiting your liability" and adjust their fee or terms
- Internal contracting decisions (which contractor does what) are our business, not theirs to confirm

## Application

When drafting appointment/contract emails:
1. Reference the subcontractor's own signed-off SOW document
2. List their deliverables from the submittal register
3. Request their confirmation of acceptance — no need to mention who does the rest

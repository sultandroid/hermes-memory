## 1. CONTRACTOR'S OBLIGATIONS (Clause 4)

### 4.1 Contractor's General Obligations
- Design (to extent specified), execute, complete Works per Contract + Engineer's instructions; remedy defects.
- Provide all Plant, Contractor's Documents, Personnel, Goods, consumables, services.
- **Eligible source country rule:** All equipment/material/services from Bank-approved source countries.
- **Site operations:** Responsible for adequacy, stability, safety of all Site operations and construction methods.
- **Design responsibility:** Only responsible for Temporary Works design and Plant/Materials per Contract — NOT Permanent Works design unless specified.
- If Contractor designs part of Permanent Works: must submit as-built docs + O&M manuals before Taking-Over.

### 4.2 Performance Security
- Obtain at own cost within **28 days** after Letter of Acceptance.
- Form/amount/currencies per Contract Data.
- Valid until Works completed + defects remedied.
- Employer returns within **21 days** after Performance Certificate.
- **25% rule:** If Variations change Contract Price >25% in a currency, adjust Performance Security proportionally.

### 4.3 Contractor's Representative
- Appoint representative with full authority; fluent in contract language.
- Cannot be replaced without Engineer's prior consent.

### 4.4 Subcontractors
- Cannot subcontract **whole** Works.
- Contractor liable for all Subcontractor acts/defaults.
- Engineer consent needed for subcontractors (except material suppliers or those named in Contract).
- **28 days' notice** before Subcontractor's work commences.

### 4.12 Unforeseeable Physical Conditions (KEY)
- "Physical conditions" = natural physical conditions, man-made obstructions, pollutants (NOT climatic).
- If Contractor encounters **Unforeseeable** adverse conditions: notice **as soon as practicable**.
- **Entitlement:** EOT + Cost (NOT profit).
- Engineer may offset against more favourable conditions elsewhere — but net effect cannot reduce Contract Price.
- **Museum relevance:** Archaeological finds, unexpected ground conditions, existing structures/foundations.

### 4.18 Protection of the Environment
- All reasonable steps to protect environment, limit pollution/noise/nuisance.
- Emissions, discharges, effluent must not exceed specified values.
- **Museum relevance:** Strict environmental controls near sensitive exhibits.

### 4.21 Progress Reports
- Monthly reports; first covers first calendar month after Commencement Date.
- Include: progress charts, photos, manufacturing status, quality docs, safety stats, actual vs planned comparison, events jeopardising completion.

### 4.24 Fossils (MUSEUM-SPECIFIC)
- All fossils, coins, articles of value/antiquity, geological/archaeological items found on Site belong to Employer.
- Contractor must prevent removal/damage.
- **Promptly notify** Engineer upon discovery.
- **Entitlement:** EOT + Cost from complying with instructions.
- **Museum relevance:** Museum construction often on culturally sensitive land.

## 2. VARIATIONS / CHANGE ORDERS (Clause 13)

### 13.1 Right to Vary
- Engineer may initiate Variations **any time** before Taking-Over Certificate.
- Contractor **must execute** each Variation unless cannot readily obtain required Goods (prompt notice with particulars).
- Variations may include: quantity changes, quality/characteristic changes, level/position/dimension changes, omissions (unless by others), additional work/Plant/Materials/services, sequence/timing changes.
- Contractor shall NOT alter Permanent Works without Engineer's instruction/approval.

### 13.2 Value Engineering
- Contractor may submit proposals to: accelerate completion, reduce cost, improve efficiency/value, benefit Employer.
- If approved proposal includes design change and reduces contract value: Contractor gets **50% of difference** between reduction in contract value and reduction in value to Employer.

### 13.3 Variation Procedure
- If Engineer requests proposal: respond ASAP with (a) work description + programme, (b) programme/time modifications, (c) evaluation proposal.
- **Contractor shall not delay work** while awaiting response.
- Each Variation instruction in writing; Contractor acknowledges receipt.
- Evaluated per Clause 12 (Measurement and Evaluation).

### 13.5 Provisional Sums
- Used only per Engineer's instructions.
- Nominated Subcontractor work: actual amounts paid + overhead/profit percentage.
- Contractor must produce quotations, invoices, vouchers when required.

### 13.6 Daywork
- Minor/incidental work valued per Daywork Schedule.
- Daily statements: personnel names/occupations/time, equipment ID/type/time, Plant/Materials quantities.

### 13.7 Changes in Legislation
- Contract Price adjusted for Laws changes after Base Date.
- **Entitlement:** EOT + Cost.

### 13.8 Changes in Cost (Price Adjustment)
- Formula: **Pn = a + b(Ln/Lo) + c(En/Eo) + d(Mn/Mo) + ...**
- Applies if table of adjustment data exists in Schedules.
- If Contractor fails to complete within Time for Completion: adjustment uses whichever is more favourable to Employer.

## 3. CLAIMS AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION (Clause 20)

### 20.1 Contractor's Claims (CRITICAL — STRICT TIME BARS)
- **28-day notice:** Notice to Engineer within 28 days of becoming aware of claim event.
- **CONSEQUENCE OF FAILURE:** If notice not given within 28 days → Time for Completion NOT extended, Contractor NOT entitled to additional payment, Employer **discharged from all liability**.
- **42-day detailed claim:** Within 42 days of awareness, submit fully detailed claim with full supporting particulars.
- **Continuing effect:** Interim claims monthly; final claim within 28 days after effects end.
- **Contemporary records:** Must keep records to substantiate claim; Engineer may inspect.
- **Engineer response:** Within 42 days — approval, disapproval with comments, or request further particulars.
- **Payment:** Each Payment Certificate includes reasonably substantiated claim amounts.

### 20.2 Appointment of Dispute Board (DB)
- Disputes referred to DB for decision.
- DB: 1 or 3 suitably qualified persons, fluent in contract language, experienced in construction type and contract interpretation.
- Each Party pays **one-half** of DB remuneration.
- DB appointment expires when discharge under 14.12 becomes effective.

### 20.4 Obtaining DB Decision
- Either Party may refer dispute in writing to DB.
- DB gives reasoned decision within **84 days** of receiving reference.
- Decision **binding** unless revised in amicable settlement or arbitral award.
- **28-day dissatisfaction window:** Notice within 28 days of receiving decision.
- If no dissatisfaction notice within 28 days: decision becomes **final and binding**.

### 20.5 Amicable Settlement
- After dissatisfaction notice, Parties attempt amicable settlement.
- Arbitration may commence on or after **56th day** after dissatisfaction notice.

### 20.6 Arbitration
- Default: ICC Rules of Arbitration, three arbitrators.
- Arbitrators have full power to open up, review, revise any certificate, determination, instruction, DB decision.
- Arbitration may commence **before or after** completion of Works.

### 20.7-20.8 Failure to Comply / Expiry of DB
- Failure to comply with final/binding DB decision → refer directly to arbitration.
- If no DB in place when dispute arises → refer directly to arbitration.

## 4. TIME EXTENSIONS (Clause 8)

### 8.2 Time for Completion
- Complete whole Works and each Section within Time for Completion, including passing Tests on Completion.

### 8.3 Programme
- Submit detailed time programme within **28 days** after Commencement Date notice.
- Include: order of works, stages for design/procurement/manufacture/delivery/construction/erection/testing, inspection/test sequence, supporting report with methods and resource estimates.
- Engineer has **21 days** to object; if no objection, Contractor proceeds per programme.
- Must promptly notify Engineer of specific probable future events that may adversely affect work, increase Price, or delay execution.

### 8.4 Extension of Time for Completion (KEY)
- Contractor entitled to EOT if completion delayed by:
  - **(a) Variation** (unless time adjustment already agreed)
  - **(b) Cause of delay** giving entitlement under any Sub-Clause
  - **(c) Exceptionally adverse climatic conditions**
  - **(d) Unforeseeable shortages** in personnel/Goods from epidemic or governmental actions
  - **(e) Any delay, impediment, or prevention** caused by/attributable to Employer, Employer's Personnel, or other contractors
- Notice required per 20.1 (28-day rule).
- Engineer may **increase but shall not decrease** total extension of time.

### 8.5 Delays Caused by Authorities
- If Contractor diligently followed procedures, authorities delay/disrupt work, delay was Unforeseeable → treated as cause under 8.4(b).

### 8.6 Rate of Progress
- If progress too slow (not EOT causes): Engineer may instruct revised programme and methods at Contractor's own risk and cost.

### 8.7 Delay Damages
- Daily rate per Contract Data; **maximum amount** cap applies.
- These are the **only damages** for delay (except termination).

### 8.8-8.12 Suspension
- Engineer may instruct suspension; Contractor protects/stores/secures Works.
- If suspension >84 days: Contractor may request permission to proceed. If no permission within 28 days → treat as omission or (if whole Works) terminate.

## 5. PAYMENT APPLICATIONS (Clause 14)

### 14.1 The Contract Price
- Subject to measurement/evaluation (Clause 12) and adjustments.
- Contractor pays all taxes, duties, fees (except legislation changes).
- Bill of Quantities quantities are **estimated**.
- Within **28 days** of Commencement Date: submit breakdown of lump sum prices.

### 14.2 Advance Payment
- Interest-free mobilisation loan against guarantee.
- Repaid through percentage deductions from interim payments.
- Deductions commence when certified payments exceed 30% of Accepted Contract Amount (less Provisional Sums).
- Must be fully repaid before 80% of Accepted Contract Amount certified.

### 14.3 Application for Interim Payment Certificates
- Monthly Statement in six copies.
- Includes: (a) estimated contract value of Works executed + Documents, (b) legislation/cost adjustments, (c) retention deduction, (d) advance payment, (e) Plant/Materials, (f) other additions/deductions (including claims), (g) previous certificates deduction.

### 14.6 Issue of Interim Payment Certificates
- Engineer issues IPC within **28 days** after receiving Statement.
- No amount certified until Performance Security received and approved.
- Engineer may withhold for non-conforming work or unperformed obligations.

### 14.7 Payment
- Advance payment: **42 days** after LOA or **21 days** after security (whichever later).
- IPC amounts: **56 days** after Engineer receives Statement.
- Final Payment Certificate: **56 days** after Employer receives it.

### 14.8 Delayed Payment
- Financing charges compounded monthly at **3 percentage points above** central bank discount rate.
- Entitled without formal notice or certification.

### 14.9 Payment of Retention Money
- **First half (50%):** Certified when Taking-Over Certificate issued.
- **Second half (50%):** Certified promptly after Defects Notification Period expiry.
- For Sections: 40% proportional at Section Taking-Over, balance at Section DNP expiry.
- **Retention Money guarantee alternative:** After first half released, may substitute guarantee for second half.

### 14.10 Statement at Completion
- Within **84 days** after Taking-Over Certificate: submit Statement at completion.

### 14.11-14.13 Final Payment
- Within **56 days** after Performance Certificate: submit draft final statement.
- Discharge confirms full and final settlement.
- Engineer issues Final Payment Certificate within **28 days** after receiving Final Statement + discharge.

### 14.14 Cessation of Employer's Liability
- Employer not liable unless expressly included in Final Statement and (except post-Taking-Over matters) in Statement at Completion.
- Does NOT limit liability for fraud, deliberate default, or reckless misconduct.

## 6. DEFECTS LIABILITY (Clause 11)

### 11.1 Completion of Outstanding Work and Remedying Defects
- Complete outstanding work and remedy notified defects by DNP expiry date.
- Defects notified by Employer on or before DNP expiry.

### 11.2 Cost of Remedying Defects
- At Contractor's risk and cost if attributable to: (a) Contractor's design, (b) non-conforming Plant/Materials/workmanship, (c) failure to comply with any obligation.
- If attributable to **any other cause**: treated as Variation (13.3) — Contractor gets paid.

### 11.3 Extension of Defects Notification Period
- Employer may extend DNP if Works/Section/major Plant cannot be used for intended purpose due to defect/damage.
- **Maximum extension: 2 years.**

### 11.4 Failure to Remedy Defects
- If Contractor fails to remedy within reasonable time, Employer may:
  - (a) Carry out work at Contractor's cost
  - (b) Require reasonable Contract Price reduction
  - (c) If defect deprives Employer of substantially whole benefit → **terminate Contract** and recover all sums paid + financing + dismantling costs.

### 11.9 Performance Certificate
- Engineer issues within **28 days** after latest DNP expiry (or when all work completed and defects remedied).
- **Only the Performance Certificate constitutes acceptance of the Works.**

### 11.10 Unfulfilled Obligations
- After Performance Certificate, each Party remains liable for unperformed obligations.

## 7. TERMINATION (Clauses 15 & 16)

### 15.1 Notice to Correct
- If Contractor fails any obligation, Engineer may by notice require remedy within specified reasonable time.

### 15.2 Termination by Employer
- Employer may terminate (14 days' notice) if Contractor:
  - (a) Fails Performance Security or Notice to Correct
  - (b) Abandons Works
  - (c) Fails due expedition or rejection/remedial work notices within 28 days
  - (d) Subcontracts whole Works or assigns without agreement
  - (e) Becomes bankrupt/insolvent
  - (f) Engages in corrupt/fraudulent practices
- Immediate termination for (e) or (f).
- Contractor leaves Site, delivers Goods and Documents to Engineer.

### 15.5 Termination for Convenience
- Employer may terminate at any time for convenience (28 days' notice).
- **Cannot** use to execute Works themselves, arrange another contractor, or avoid Contractor's termination under 16.2.
- Contractor paid per 19.6.

### 15.6 Corrupt or Fraudulent Practices
- Employer may terminate (14 days' notice) for corrupt, fraudulent, collusive, or coercive practices.

### 16.1 Contractor's Entitlement to Suspend Work
- If Engineer fails to certify or Employer fails to pay: suspend after **21 days' notice**.
- If Bank suspends loan and no alternative funds: suspend with **7 days' notice**.
- **Entitlement:** EOT + Cost + profit.

### 16.2 Termination by Contractor
- Contractor may terminate (14 days' notice) if:
  - (a) No financial arrangement evidence within 42 days of 16.1 notice
  - (b) Engineer fails Payment Certificate within 56 days of Statement
  - (c) Payment not received within 42 days of due date
  - (d) Employer substantially fails obligations, materially affecting Contractor
  - (e) Employer fails Contract Agreement or Assignment rules
  - (f) Prolonged suspension >84 days affecting whole Works
  - (g) Employer bankrupt/insolvent
  - (h) Bank suspends loan and Contractor hasn't received sums within 14 days

### 16.4 Payment on Termination (by Contractor)
- Employer must promptly: (a) return Performance Security, (b) pay per 19.6, (c) pay loss/damage sustained by Contractor.

## 8. KEY PRACTICAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR MUSEUM PROJECTS

### Museum-Specific Risk Areas

| Risk Area | Clause | Action Required |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Archaeological finds | 4.24 | Immediate notice to Engineer; claim EOT + Cost |
| Environmental sensitivity | 4.18 | Dust/vibration/noise control plans; monitor emissions |
| Unforeseeable ground conditions | 4.12 | Notice as soon as practicable; keep contemporary records |
| Design changes for exhibit spaces | 13.1/13.3 | Formal Variation procedure; don't proceed without instruction |
| Value engineering for museum fit-out | 13.2 | Submit proposals; 50% share of savings |
| Strict completion dates (exhibition openings) | 8.4/8.7 | Proactive EOT claims; monitor delay damage exposure |
| Specialist subcontractors (display cases, AV) | 4.4/5 | Engineer consent needed; 28 days' notice |
| Fossils/antiquities discovery | 4.24 | Stop work in area; notify Engineer; claim time/cost |

### Critical Notice Requirements (Time Bars)

| Trigger | Deadline | Clause |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Notice of claim (EOT or additional payment) | **28 days** from awareness | 20.1 |
| Fully detailed claim | **42 days** from awareness | 20.1 |
| Notice of Force Majeure | **14 days** from awareness | 19.2 |
| Notice of dissatisfaction with DB decision | **28 days** from receipt | 20.4 |
| Commence arbitration after dissatisfaction | After **56 days** | 20.5/20.6 |
| Notice of Variation inability to obtain Goods | **Promptly** | 13.1 |
| Notice of Engineer's delayed drawings | **Before** delay occurs | 1.9 |
| Notice of unforeseen physical conditions | **As soon as practicable** | 4.12 |
| Notice of fossils/archaeological finds | **Promptly** upon discovery | 4.24 |

### Payment Timeline Summary

| Payment Event | Timeline | Clause |
|---------------|----------|--------|
| Advance Payment | 42 days after LOA or 21 days after security | 14.7(a) |
| Interim Payment Certificate | 28 days after Statement | 14.6 |
| Payment of IPC amount | 56 days after Engineer receives Statement | 14.7(b) |
| First half Retention Money | At Taking-Over Certificate | 14.9 |
| Second half Retention Money | After DNP expiry | 14.9 |
| Statement at Completion | 84 days after Taking-Over Certificate | 14.10 |
| Final Payment Certificate | 28 days after Final Statement + Discharge | 14.13 |
| Financing charges for late payment | 3% above central bank discount rate | 14.8 |

### Dispute Resolution Ladder

1. **Engineer's Determination** (3.5) — first step for most matters
2. **Dispute Board** (20.2-20.4) — 84 days for decision; binding unless challenged
3. **Amicable Settlement** (20.5) — 56-day window
4. **Arbitration** (20.6) — ICC Rules, 3 arbitrators, final and binding
